How to order DfE laptops, tablets and routers

How to order laptops, tablets and routers through the Get help with technology service.

Find out who can get laptops and tablets.

  1. Step 1 Get set up to order

    DfE asked local authorities (LAs) and academy trusts to use the Get help with technology service to tell us if they wanted to:

    • let schools place their own orders
    • place orders centrally on behalf of schools.

    If you’ve been nominated to place an order, you should have received an email inviting you to the Get help with technology service where you can:

    • amend existing contact information
    • add additional contacts to place orders
    • view your device allocation.

    If you need access to use the service, read our guidance.

    About device allocation

    Each school has an allocation figure. This is the total number of devices they can order. There’s also a ‘cap’ figure for each school, which represents the number of devices they can order at any one time, to help manage stock levels.

    Where a responsible body, for example an academy trust, orders on behalf of several schools, they’ll have a ‘shared’ cap.

    For example, the responsible body may have an allocation of 100 devices across 4 schools. To begin with, all 4 schools will show an allocation of 100 devices. If the responsible body then orders 25 devices for 1 school, all 4 will now show an allocation of 75.

    Learn more about how devices are allocated.

  2. Step 2 Choose devices and provide technical information

    We may need some technical information from you before you can order, so we recommend choosing the types of devices you’ll need before we send out your invitation to order.

    Check the technical specifications of the laptops and tablets provided by the Get help with technology programme.

    The device types (depending on availability) are:

    • Microsoft Windows laptops and tablets
    • Google Chromebooks
    • Apple iPads

    You can also order 4G wireless routers, but you will need to supply your own SIM cards and data.

    Important information about Microsoft Windows laptops and tablets

    Windows devices are provided with factory settings. Schools, colleges, further education providers, trusts or LAs should configure them at their own cost before lending them to students. This means installing preferred safeguarding measures and bringing them into an existing device management framework.

    Read our guidance on preparing a standard Windows device.

    Important information about Google Chromebooks

    You need to sign in to the Get help with technology service and provide the following information before you can order Google Chromebooks:

    • the domain the school, college or FE provider has registered for Google Workspace for Education Fundamentals (formerly G Suite for Education), for example ‘school.co.uk’
    • a recovery email address on a separate domain to the one registered for Google Workspace for Education Fundamentals – for example, if the registered domain is ‘school.com’, the recovery email cannot be ‘recovery@school.com’.

    If you’re not sure how to find this information, you should ask your IT support for help.

    If the school or college you’re ordering for does not currently use an education platform, you can apply for government-funded support to get access to either a Google Workspace for Education Fundamentals or Microsoft 365 Education platform. The closing date for all applications is 30 November 2021.

    Read more information about preparing Google Chromebooks for pupils and students.

    Important information about Apple iPads

    When you order iPads we’ll ask for your Apple School Manager (ASM). If you need to find this, you can follow these steps:

    1. Log in to Apple School Manager.
    2. Select ‘Settings’, then select ‘Enrolment Information’.
    3. Look for your Organisation ID in the Enrolment Information panel.

    Find out how to enrol in Apple School Manager.

    View the full specifications of all of these devices.

    To help organisations with the cost of technical support to set up these new devices, we’re offering a devices grant. Learn more about the devices grant, including the eligibility criteria.

  3. Step 3 Order devices when you’re invited to

    You’ll be asked to sign in to the Get help with technology service to check your allocation. You’ll then be taken to our technology partner’s website to place your order.

    Once you select the devices you want to order, you’ll be asked to confirm delivery and contact details.

  4. Step 4 Receive your order

    Most devices will be delivered within 5 working days of an order being confirmed, subject to stock availability. For large orders (more than 150 devices), we’ll call the main delivery contact to arrange delivery.

    Find out about ownership, and options for lending or giving devices to children, young people or other organisations.

    Find out how to set up your devices and what to do if you have a faulty device.
